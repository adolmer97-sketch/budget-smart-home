Living in an apartment means working within your landlord's rules. You cannot rewire outlets, swap light switches, or drill holes for a hardwired security system. That is exactly why smart plugs are the single best smart home device for renters. They plug into any standard outlet, require zero modifications, and come with you when your lease is up.

We went through our full smart plug rankings and pulled out the three models that make the most sense for apartment dwellers. Below you will find our top picks, tips for getting the most out of smart plugs in a rental, and a simple setup plan to start automating your apartment today.

Why Smart Plugs Are Perfect for Apartment Living

Smart plugs solve a real problem for renters: how do you make your home smarter without making permanent changes? Here is why they are the ideal starting point.

Zero Installation Required

Smart plugs do not require any tools, wiring, or mounting hardware. You plug them into an existing outlet, connect them to your Wi-Fi network through a phone app, and they are ready to go. There is nothing to patch, paint, or repair when you move out.

Completely Portable

Unlike smart light switches or hardwired sensors, smart plugs travel with you. When your lease ends, unplug them, toss them in a box, and set them up at your next place in minutes. Your schedules and automations carry over through the app.

Affordable Entry Point

Most budget smart plugs cost between $6 and $15. Even if you outfit every room in a one-bedroom apartment, you are looking at $30 to $60 total. That is less than a single month of most streaming subscriptions, and the energy savings from scheduling devices can actually pay for the plugs over time.

Landlord-Friendly by Default

Because smart plugs use existing outlets and do not alter the electrical system, there is nothing for your landlord to object to. You are not modifying their property in any way. This is not the case with smart switches, smart thermostats, or wired security cameras, all of which may require permission or lease modifications.

Apartment Smart Plug Setup Tips

Getting smart plugs working well in an apartment is straightforward, but a few extra steps will help you get the most value out of them.

Start with High-Impact Locations

Focus on the devices you interact with most. A lamp in the living room, a fan in the bedroom, and a coffee maker in the kitchen are three easy wins that make a noticeable difference in your daily routine. You can always expand later.

Use Schedules to Save Energy

Set your smart plugs to turn off entertainment centers, chargers, and other devices that draw phantom power overnight. Many electronics consume small amounts of electricity even when they appear to be off. Over a year, those savings add up, especially on a renter's budget.

Create a Leaving-Home Routine

Group all your smart plugs into a single voice command or app routine. When you leave for work, a single tap or voice command turns everything off. When you come home, another command turns on your living room lamp and kitchen lights. This works especially well in small apartments where you can reach everything from one routine.

Check Your Wi-Fi Coverage

Apartment Wi-Fi can be unreliable if your router is in a far corner or if neighboring networks cause interference. Smart plugs need a stable 2.4GHz Wi-Fi signal to work reliably. If your plug is in a room far from the router, consider a Wi-Fi extender or a mesh network. Most budget mesh systems cost around $60 to $80 and cover a typical apartment easily.
