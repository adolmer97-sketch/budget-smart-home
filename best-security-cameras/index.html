Buying Guide: What to Look for in a Budget Security Camera

Resolution

At minimum, look for 1080p (Full HD) resolution. This provides enough detail to identify faces and read license plates at reasonable distances. If your budget allows, 2K cameras like the Eufy C24 offer a meaningful upgrade in clarity. Avoid anything below 1080p in 2025 as the image quality will leave you frustrated when you actually need to review footage.

Night Vision

Most budget cameras include infrared night vision, which produces a black-and-white image in the dark. This is perfectly functional for security purposes. Color night vision, found in the Wyze Cam v3, uses a more sensitive sensor to capture color detail in low light. This makes it easier to identify clothing colors and vehicle details. If your camera will monitor a dimly lit area, color night vision is worth prioritizing.

Storage Options

Security camera storage falls into three categories: local (microSD card), cloud (subscription-based), and hybrid (both options). Local storage has no recurring costs but risks losing footage if the camera is stolen or damaged. Cloud storage provides remote access and backup but typically requires a monthly subscription. We recommend cameras that offer at least local storage so you are never forced into a paid plan.

Weather Resistance

If you plan to mount a camera outdoors, check for an IP65 or higher weather resistance rating. The Wyze Cam v3 is the only camera on our list rated for outdoor use. Indoor-only cameras can be damaged by moisture, temperature extremes, and dust. For outdoor monitoring on a budget, a weather-resistant camera saves you from replacing a damaged unit down the road.

Smart Home Compatibility

Consider which voice assistant and smart home platform you already use. Alexa users will appreciate the tight integration of the Blink Mini 2. Apple HomeKit users should look at the Eufy C24. Google Assistant support is available on the AOSU Mini and others. Choosing a camera that fits your existing ecosystem means smoother setup and more useful automations like turning on lights when motion is detected.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do budget security cameras work without Wi-Fi?

Most budget security cameras require a Wi-Fi connection for live viewing, notifications, and cloud storage. However, cameras with microSD card slots (like the Wyze Cam v3 and Tapo C200) can record footage locally even if Wi-Fi drops temporarily. You will not receive real-time alerts without an internet connection, but the footage will be saved on the card for later review.

Are budget security cameras safe from hackers?

Any internet-connected device carries some risk, but you can minimize it significantly. Use a strong, unique password for your camera account, enable two-factor authentication when available, and keep the camera firmware updated. Cameras with local-only storage like the Eufy C24 reduce exposure by keeping footage off cloud servers entirely.

Can I use a budget security camera outdoors?

Only if the camera is rated for outdoor use. On our list, the Wyze Cam v3 has an IP65 weather resistance rating, making it suitable for covered and exposed outdoor locations. Using an indoor-only camera outside will likely result in damage from rain, humidity, or temperature swings. If you need outdoor coverage, choose a model specifically designed for it.

How much cloud storage do security camera subscriptions cost?

Cloud storage plans typically range from $2 to $10 per month per camera. Wyze Cam Plus costs about $2 per month, while Blink subscriptions start around $3 per month. Many cameras on our list offer free local storage via microSD cards, which can eliminate subscription costs entirely. We recommend starting with local storage and adding cloud backup only if you find you need remote access to recordings.

What size microSD card should I use with my security camera?

A 32 GB card provides roughly 2 to 4 days of continuous 1080p recording, while a 128 GB card extends that to about 10 to 14 days depending on the camera's compression. For most users, a 64 GB or 128 GB card offers a good balance between storage capacity and cost. Check your camera's maximum supported card size before purchasing, as some models cap at 128 GB or 256 GB.
