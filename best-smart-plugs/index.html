Why Smart Plugs Are the Easiest Way to Start Your Smart Home

If you have ever wanted to dip your toes into the smart home world without committing to a full ecosystem, smart plugs are the answer. They require zero wiring, work with your existing devices, and can be set up in under five minutes. Plug one into any outlet, connect it to your Wi-Fi, and suddenly that old floor lamp or window fan becomes a voice-controlled smart device. At prices starting under $10, there is simply no cheaper or easier entry point into home automation.

We spent weeks researching and comparing dozens of budget smart plugs to find the five that deliver the best combination of reliability, app quality, voice assistant support, and overall value. Every pick on this list costs under $30, and most come in well below $15. Here are our top recommendations for 2025.

Buying Guide: What to Look for in a Budget Smart Plug

Not all smart plugs are created equal, even at the budget end. Here are the five key factors to consider before buying.

Voice Assistant Compatibility

The first thing to check is whether a plug works with your voice assistant of choice. Most budget plugs support Alexa and Google Assistant, but Apple HomeKit support is much rarer and usually costs a bit more. If you are an iPhone user who relies on Siri and the Apple Home app, narrow your search to HomeKit-compatible options like the Meross MSS110.

Physical Size and Design

A smart plug that blocks the second outlet on a wall plate is a deal breaker for many people. Look for compact or slim-profile designs that leave the adjacent outlet free. This is especially important if you plan to use two smart plugs on the same wall plate or if your outlets are in tight spaces behind furniture.

Energy Monitoring

Some smart plugs can track how much electricity your connected devices consume. This is a genuinely useful feature if you want to identify energy hogs or estimate your monthly electricity costs. At the budget level, energy monitoring is uncommon, but the Meross MSS110 includes it at just $13.

App Quality

You will interact with your smart plug's app more than you think, especially when setting up schedules, creating automations, or grouping plugs by room. A well-designed app like Kasa makes daily use pleasant, while a bare-bones app can make even simple tasks frustrating. Read a few app reviews before committing to a specific brand.

Price and Value

Budget smart plugs range from about $6 to $25. At the low end, you get basic on/off control and voice support. As you move up, you gain features like energy monitoring, better app experiences, and broader compatibility. Consider how many plugs you need: if you are outfitting an entire house, a $6 plug that works well enough might save you far more than a $25 plug with a few extra features.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do smart plugs use a lot of electricity on their own?

No. Most smart plugs consume between 0.5 and 1 watt of power while on standby, which amounts to less than $1 per year in electricity costs. The energy savings you gain from scheduling and automating devices typically far outweigh the tiny amount of power the plug itself draws.

Can I use a smart plug with a power strip or extension cord?

Yes, you can plug a smart plug into a power strip, and it will control everything connected to that strip as a single group. However, you should never plug a power strip into a smart plug if the combined load exceeds the plug's rated wattage, which is typically around 1,800 watts or 15 amps for most models. Always check the specs before connecting high-draw appliances.

Do smart plugs work without Wi-Fi?

Most budget smart plugs require an active Wi-Fi connection to function. If your internet goes down, you will typically lose voice control and app access, though some plugs retain their last scheduled state. A few higher-end plugs support Bluetooth as a fallback, but this is rare at the budget level.

Are budget smart plugs safe to use?

Yes, as long as you buy from reputable brands and check for safety certifications like UL, ETL, or FCC. All five plugs on our list are from established manufacturers with proper certifications. Avoid no-name plugs from unknown sellers, as they may lack proper safety testing and could pose a fire risk.

Can I use a smart plug with a space heater or air conditioner?

It depends on the wattage. Most budget smart plugs support up to 1,800 watts (15 amps), which covers most small space heaters and portable air conditioners. However, some manufacturers explicitly advise against using smart plugs with heating appliances due to safety concerns. Always check both the plug's and the appliance's ratings, and never exceed the plug's maximum load.
