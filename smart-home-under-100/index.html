Smart Speaker ($28) — The Brain of Your Setup

The Amazon Echo Dot 5th Gen is the single most important piece of a budget smart home. It is what you talk to when you want to control your lights, plugs, and camera. Without it, you would need to open a phone app every time you want to toggle a device, which defeats much of the convenience.

Why the Echo Dot over alternatives?

At $28, the Echo Dot is the most affordable name-brand smart speaker with broad device compatibility. It works with virtually every smart home product on the market through Alexa. The Google Nest Mini is a solid alternative at $30, but the Echo Dot edges it out on device compatibility and routine features. If you already own a Google speaker, use that instead and save $28.

What you can do with it

  • Control every other device on this list by voice
  • Set morning and bedtime routines that trigger multiple devices

  • Stream music, news, and podcasts
  • Make hands-free phone calls

Step-by-Step Setup Guide

Step 1: Set Up the Echo Dot (10 minutes)

Plug in the Echo Dot, download the Alexa app on your phone, and follow the in-app instructions to connect it to your Wi-Fi. Once connected, give it a name like "Living Room Echo" so voice commands are clear.

Step 2: Connect the Smart Plugs (15 minutes)

Plug each Wyze Plug into an outlet, download the Wyze app, and pair each plug to your Wi-Fi. Then enable the Wyze skill in the Alexa app so you can control the plugs by voice. Name each plug after its location and connected device, like "Kitchen Coffee Maker" or "Bedroom Fan."

Step 3: Install the Smart Bulbs (10 minutes)

Screw each Govee bulb into a lamp socket, download the Govee app, and pair them to Wi-Fi. Enable the Govee skill in Alexa and name each bulb by room. Test voice commands to make sure "Alexa, turn on the bedroom light" works correctly.

Step 4: Position the Camera (10 minutes)

Place the Wyze Cam v3 near your front door on a shelf or flat surface. Insert a microSD card for local recording. Pair it through the Wyze app and adjust motion detection sensitivity to avoid false alerts from passing cars or pets.

Step 5: Create Routines (10 minutes)

In the Alexa app, create two basic routines. A "Good Morning" routine that turns on the kitchen plug (coffee maker) and sets the living room bulb to bright white. A "Good Night" routine that turns off all plugs and dims all bulbs to off. These two routines alone transform the daily experience of your smart home.
